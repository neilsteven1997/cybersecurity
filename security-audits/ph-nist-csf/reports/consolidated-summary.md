# Consolidated Summary – PH NIST CSF 2.0 Hypothetical Audits

**Date:** March 2026  
**Purpose:** High-level comparison of four NIST CSF 2.0 profiles tailored to Philippine contexts.

## Profiles Overview

| Profile                  | Type              | Main Focus Areas                          | Maturity Level (Hypothetical) | Key Gaps / Priorities                  |
|--------------------------|-------------------|-------------------------------------------|-------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| Philippine Government    | Organizational   | GOVERN, supply chain, critical infra     | Partial (many policies exist but uneven enforcement) | Stronger governance, national coordination, incident reporting |
| Private Companies        | Organizational   | IDENTIFY (risk), PROTECT (access), DETECT | Partial to Low (varies by company size) | Supply chain security, employee training, basic monitoring |
| Filipino People          | Community        | Basic personal protection, awareness     | Low (most citizens lack fundamentals) | Password hygiene, 2FA, phishing awareness campaigns |
| Botium Toys              | Small Business   | Online shop security, customer data      | Low (minimal controls in place) | Access control, backups, vulnerability patching |

## Cross-Profile Observations

- **Common strengths** — Most profiles show some awareness of IDENTIFY (assets/data) and PROTECT basics.
- **Common weaknesses** — Limited DETECT/RESPOND capabilities across all groups; weak governance in non-government sectors.
- **Philippine-specific themes** — Data Privacy Act (RA 10173) compliance is a shared driver; supply chain risks appear in government and private profiles.
- **Biggest opportunity** — National awareness campaigns could lift Filipino People and small businesses (like Botium Toys) quickly.

## Overall Recommendations
1. Prioritize **GOVERN** function for government → cascade policies downward.
2. Focus on **basic hygiene** (passwords, 2FA, updates) for citizens and small orgs.
3. Encourage **private sector** to adopt community baselines from government profiles.
4. Use free NIST tools (Reference Tool, Community Profiles) for future updates.

See individual profile folders in `/profiles/` for detailed audits, recommendations, and evidence.
