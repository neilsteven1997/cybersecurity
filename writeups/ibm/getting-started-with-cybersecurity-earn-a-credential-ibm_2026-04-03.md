Key elements of cybersecurity

Cybersecurity involves multiple layers of protection. Organizations must consider three key elements: people, processes, and technology.

Select each tab to learn more about the elements of cybersecurity.

People are the most essential part of cybersecurity. You might have guessed that technology is more important. But remember that people are the ones using it. They’re also the ones responsible for its security. They design and maintain systems, and they monitor security messages and alarms.


For these reasons, employees at all levels of an organization need to understand why cybersecurity is so important. They also need to understand their role in protecting the organization’s systems and information. Intentionally or not, any employee could be the weak link that provides an attacker an opportunity to access the network. Employees need cybersecurity training appropriate for their job and industry and must take cybersecurity seriously. 


A good cybersecurity approach anticipates and allows for human behavior.

Processes 


In organizations, most activities follow clearly defined processes. These processes should include security at each step. But if they’re too complicated, users get frustrated.


Good processes have the following traits:

    They’re clear and easy to follow.
    They’re accessible and well known. If you’re expected to follow a process, you should know how to access it. For example, consider fire evacuations in buildings. Most people know where the nearest evacuation points are because of good signage.
    They’re consistent. They don’t contradict each other. Lots of exceptions or deviations increases complexity.




---

Tech

Technology includes your organization’s infrastructure and the tools for protecting it. These tools can include antimalware software, email security, and firewalls, to name a few examples.


When used correctly, technology solves problems without creating new ones for users. Take device management software, which can track software patch statuses and apply updates. This software is essential for many large organizations. If implemented correctly, the technology is non-intrusive, and you barely notice it. But if implemented poorly, the software becomes a nuisance. You might even try disabling it entirely.
